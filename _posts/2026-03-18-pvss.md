**tl;dr:** A comparison of the Schoenmakers[^Scho99] and SCRAPE[^CD17e] PVSS schemes.

<!--more-->

## Comparison

Let $(\mathbb{G}, p, \term{G}, \term{H})$ define a cyclic group $\mathbb{G}$ of prime order $p$ with two independent generators $G$ and $H$.

There are $\term{n}$ parties, each with an encryption key $\term{\mathsf{ek}_i} = \term{\mathsf{dk}_i} \cdot H$.

The dealer picks a random field element $\term{s}$ and secret-shares its **group element encoding** $\term{S} = s \cdot H$ using a degree-$\term{t}$ polynomial $\term{a(X)} = \sum_{j = 0}^t a_j X^j$ with $a_0 = a(0) = s$ and the other $a_i$'s randomly-picked.

|  | Schoenmakers[^Scho99] | SCRAPE (DLEQ)[^CD17e] | SCRAPE (pairing)[^CD17e] |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Commitments** | To polynomial **coefficients**: $C_j = a_j \cdot G$ for $j \in [0, t)$ | To polynomial **evaluations**: $V_i = a(i) \cdot G$ for $i \in [n]$ | Same as SCRAPE (DLEQ) |
| **Ciphertexts** | $\hat{S}_i = a(i) \cdot \mathsf{ek}_i$ | Same | Same |
| **Correct-encryption proof** | $\mathsf{DLEQ}(G, X_i, \mathsf{ek}_i, \hat{S}_i)$ where $X_i = \sum_j i^j \cdot C_j$ | $\mathsf{DLEQ}(G, V_i, \mathsf{ek}_i, \hat{S}_i)$ | Pairing check: $e(\hat{S}_i, G) = e(\mathsf{ek}_i, V_i)$ |
| **Verification** | Verifier recomputes $X_i = \sum_j i^j \cdot C_j$ per share: $O(nt)$ total | Dual-code check $\sum_i c_i^\perp \cdot V_i = 0$: $O(n)$ total | Same |
| **Model** | ROM (DDH) | Same | Plain model (DBS) |
| **Reconstruction** | $\tilde{S}_i = \mathsf{dk}_i^{-1} \cdot \hat{S}_i = a(i) \cdot H$, then Lagrange in $\mathbb{G}$ | Same | Same, but secret is $S = e(H, H)^s$ |

The key difference is the verification step.
Schoenmakers commits to polynomial **coefficients**, so verifying that a ciphertext $\hat{S}_i$ encrypts the correct share requires recomputing the evaluation commitment $X_i = \sum_j i^j \cdot C_j$ from the coefficient commitments, costing $O(t)$ per share and $O(nt)$ total.
SCRAPE commits to polynomial **evaluations** directly and replaces per-share verification with a single $O(n)$ dual-code check from coding theory: sample a random codeword $\mathbf{c}^\perp$ from the dual code $C^\perp$ of the Reed-Solomon code and check $\sum_i c_i^\perp \cdot V_i = 0$.
The DLEQ variant works in the ROM under DDH, while the pairing variant works in the plain model under the Decisional Bilinear Square (DBS) assumption, replacing DLEQ proofs with pairing checks $e(\hat{S}_i, G) = e(\mathsf{ek}_i, V_i)$.

## Related work

 - Groth[^Grot21e] presents a PVSS for **field elements** (not just group elements) using chunked encryption and approximate ZK range proofs
 - Das et al.[^DPTX24e] introduce a **weighted** and **aggregatable** PVSS scheme designed for proof-of-stake blockchains where a validators's stake represents their weight in the PVSS
    + Das et al. mix the SCRAPE PVSS with ideas from Groth's PVSS (i.e., ElGamal encryption) to speed up the verification time
 - [Chunky](/chunky): A work-in-progress weighted PVSS for **field elements** (rather than group elements) using chunked [ElGamal](/elgamal) encryption and [DeKART](/dekart) range proofs.
