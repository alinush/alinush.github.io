**tl;dr:** 
**UTT** is Chaumiam ecash done the **right way**: efficiently, decentralized and with **sensible-anonymity**: a user can only send (say) $\\$L$ coins per month (assuming a Sybil-resistant proof-of-humanity system).
UTT is academic work that started in 2018 at VMware, was rebooted in 2021 and now lives as an eprint[^TBAplus22e].
UTT did see deployment in a testing pilot with Israel's central bank[^ref1]$^,$[^ref2].
Below, I'll post some links to some slides and talks.
Hopefully this will become a full blog post later.

## Blogposts

UTT relies on many cryptographic building blocks. Here are relevant blog posts:

**Cryptographic primitives at the core of UTT:**
 - [Pedersen commitments](/pedersen) — coins are represented as Pedersen commitments to (pid, sn, value)
 - [Blind and threshold signatures](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2026-01-21-blind-and-threshold/) — Chaum's blind signatures are the foundation of ecash
 - [Pointcheval-Sanders (PS) signatures](/pointcheval-sanders) — used as the rerandomizable signature scheme for signing coins
 - [Sigma protocols](/sigma) — used for proving correctness of nullifiers and authorizing spends
 - [Zero-knowledge proofs](/zkps) — the foundation for transaction privacy
 - [Anonymous credentials](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2023-01-08-re-rand-cred/) — UTT's registration credentials are a form of anonymous credential
 - [Range proofs from polynomial commitments](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2020-03-03-range-proofs-from-polynomial-commitments-reexplained/) — an alternative approach to range proofs
 - [DeKART range proofs](/dekart) — used to prove value preservation (no money creation)
 - [Identity-based encryption (IBE)](/ibe) — used to encrypt coin values to recipients using user-friendly identifiers (e.g., phone numbers)
 - [ElGamal encryption](/elgamal) — related encryption primitive
 - [Polynomial secret sharing](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2020-07-17-polynomial-secret-sharing-and-the-lagrange-basis/) — background on Shamir secret sharing used in threshold cryptography

**Relevant blog posts from [decentralizedthoughts.github.io](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io):**
 - [A simple zero-knowledge proof](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2020-12-08-a-simple-and-succinct-zero-knowledge-proof/) — introductory ZK proof concepts
 - [Simple anonymous payments](https://decentralizedthoughts.github.io/2026-01-21-simple-payment/) — a simplified model of anonymous payment systems
