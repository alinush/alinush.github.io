**tl;dr:** Encrypt the message _twice_, under two independent public keys, and attach a NIZK proof that both ciphertexts hide the _same_ message. A sound NIZK buys you CCA1 security; a (one-time) simulation-sound NIZK buys you CCA2.

## Introduction

[Vanilla PKE](/encryption) only promises [semantic security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semantic_security) (IND-CPA): the ciphertext hides the message from a _passive_ eavesdropper.
But it says nothing about an _active_ adversary who can feed mauled ciphertexts to a decryption oracle.

The **Naor-Yung** paradigm[^NY90] is a generic way to upgrade any IND-CPA scheme into a chosen-ciphertext-secure one.
The trick is **double encryption**: encrypt $m$ under two independent public keys and prove _in zero-knowledge_ that the two ciphertexts encrypt the same plaintext.

Below, I first give the generic transform in the [PKE interface](/encryption#public-key-encryption-scheme), treating the inner IND-CPA scheme and the NIZK as black boxes.
Then I instantiate everything concretely over an abelian group $\Gr$ using [(exponentiated) ElGamal](/elgamal#exponentiated-elgamal) and a generalized-Schnorr $\Sigma$-protocol.

## Preliminaries

We need two ingredients:

* An inner PKE $\Pi = (\Pi.\kgen, \Pi.\enc, \Pi.\dec)$ that is **IND-CPA**, with message space $\msgsp$ and encryption-randomness space $\rndsp$.
* A **NIZK** $(\nizk.\setup, \nizk.\prove, \nizk.\verify)$ for the **plaintext-equality relation**:

\begin{align}
\Releq = \left\\{\big(\underbrace{(\pk_0,\pk_1,C_0,C_1)}\_{\text{statement } x};\ \underbrace{(m, r_0, r_1)}\_{\text{witness } w}\big) :
\begin{array}{l}
    C_0 = \Pi.\enc(\pk_0,m;r_0)\ \wedge\\\\\
    C_1 = \Pi.\enc(\pk_1,m;r_1)
\end{array}
\right\\}
\end{align}

where $m \in \msgsp$ and $r_0, r_1 \in \rndsp$.

> **A crucial knob.**
> With an _adaptively sound_ NIZK, the transform achieves **IND-CCA1** ("lunchtime" security) — this is the original Naor-Yung result[^NY90].
> To achieve **IND-CCA2**, the NIZK must additionally be **one-time simulation-sound**[^Saha99]: this prevents the adversary from _mauling_ the proof attached to the challenge ciphertext into a fresh, valid proof for a related ciphertext.
> The Fiat-Shamir instantiation in the [concrete section](#a-concrete-instantiation-over-an-abelian-group) gives this for free in the ROM.

## The generic Naor-Yung transform $\NY[\Pi, \nizk]$

### $\NY.\kgen(1^\lambda) \rightarrow (\sk, \pk)$

* $(\sk_0, \pk_0) \randget \Pi.\kgen(1^\lambda)$
* $(\sk_1, \pk_1) \randget \Pi.\kgen(1^\lambda)$
* $\sigma \randget \nizk.\setup(1^\lambda)$ &nbsp;&nbsp;_(the CRS)_
* $\pk \bydef (\pk_0, \pk_1, \sigma)$
* $\sk \bydef (\sk_0, \pk)$ &nbsp;&nbsp;_(only $\sk_0$ is used to decrypt; $\sk_1$ is never needed)_

### $\NY.\enc(\pk, m; (r_0, r_1)) \rightarrow C$

Here $m \in \msgsp$ and $r_0, r_1 \in \rndsp$.

* $C_0 \gets \Pi.\enc(\pk_0, m; r_0)$
* $C_1 \gets \Pi.\enc(\pk_1, m; r_1)$
* $\pi \gets \nizk.\prove\big(\sigma,\ x = (\pk_0,\pk_1,C_0,C_1),\ w = (m, r_0, r_1)\big)$
* **return** $C \bydef (C_0, C_1, \pi)$

### $\NY.\dec(\sk, C = (C_0, C_1, \pi)) \rightarrow m \text{ or } \bot$

* **if** $\nizk.\verify\big(\sigma,\ (\pk_0,\pk_1,C_0,C_1),\ \pi\big) = 0$: **return** $\bot$
* **return** $\Pi.\dec(\sk_0, C_0)$

#### Correctness

For honestly-generated keys and any $m, r_0, r_1$, the proof verifies by NIZK completeness, so $\dec$ does not abort, and $\Pi.\dec(\sk_0, C_0) = m$ by correctness of $\Pi$.

The redundancy is the whole point: by soundness of $\pi$, decrypting $C_1$ under $\sk_1$ yields the _same_ $m$.
In the CCA reduction, the simulator embeds the challenge key into (say) $\pk_0$ and keeps $\sk_1$, so it can answer every decryption query via $\Pi.\dec(\sk_1, C_1)$ without ever knowing $\sk_0$.
Simulation-soundness guarantees the adversary cannot slip in a $C$ where $C_0, C_1$ disagree.

## A concrete instantiation over an abelian group

We now plug in [exponentiated ElGamal](/elgamal#exponentiated-elgamal) for $\Pi$.

* We assume a group $\Gr$ where Decisional Diffie-Hellman (DDH) is hard.
* We use additive group notation for $\Gr$, with two public generators $G, H \in \Gr$ such that $\log_G H$ is unknown.
* Scalars live in $\F$; the message space is $\msgsp = \F$.

As on the [ElGamal page](/elgamal), an inner ciphertext is a _pair_ $(C, D) \in \Gr^2$, and decryption returns $m \cdot G$ (recover $m$ via a discrete-log algorithm when it is small).
We instantiate the NIZK via [Fiat-Shamir](/fiat-shamir), so the random oracle replaces the CRS and $\setup$ is empty.

### $\NY.\kgen(1^\lambda) \rightarrow (\sk, \pk)$

* $\dk_0, \dk_1 \randget \F$
* $\ek_0 \gets \dk_0 \cdot H$, &nbsp; $\ek_1 \gets \dk_1 \cdot H$ &nbsp;&nbsp;$(\in \Gr)$
* $\pk \bydef (\ek_0, \ek_1)$, &nbsp; $\sk \bydef (\dk_0, \pk)$

### $\NY.\enc(\pk, m; (r_0, r_1)) \rightarrow C$

Types: $m, r_0, r_1 \in \F$; all outputs in $\Gr$.

* $C_0 \gets m \cdot G + r_0 \cdot \ek_0$, &nbsp; $D_0 \gets r_0 \cdot H$
* $C_1 \gets m \cdot G + r_1 \cdot \ek_1$, &nbsp; $D_1 \gets r_1 \cdot H$
* $\pi \gets \nizk.\prove\big((\ek_0,\ek_1,C_0,D_0,C_1,D_1),\ (m,r_0,r_1)\big)$ &nbsp;&nbsp;_(see [below](#a-sigma-protocol-for-releq))_
* **return** $C \bydef (C_0, D_0, C_1, D_1, \pi)$

### $\NY.\dec(\sk, C = (C_0, D_0, C_1, D_1, \pi)) \rightarrow m \cdot G \text{ or } \bot$

* **if** $\nizk.\verify\big((\ek_0,\ek_1,C_0,D_0,C_1,D_1),\ \pi\big) = 0$: **return** $\bot$
* **return** $C_0 - \dk_0 \cdot D_0$ &nbsp;&nbsp;$(= m \cdot G)$

#### Correctness

As for [exponentiated ElGamal](/elgamal#correctness):
\begin{align}
C_0 - \dk_0 \cdot D_0
&= (m \cdot G + r_0 \cdot \ek_0) - \dk_0 \cdot (r_0 \cdot H)\\\\\
&= (m \cdot G + (r_0 \cdot \dk_0) \cdot H) - (\dk_0 \cdot r_0) \cdot H\\\\\
&= m \cdot G
\end{align}

### The plaintext-equality relation, concretely

\begin{align}
\Releq = \left\\{
\big((\ek_0,\ek_1,C_0,D_0,C_1,D_1),\ (m,r_0,r_1)\big) :
\begin{array}{l}
C_0 = m G + r_0 \ek_0 \ \wedge\ D_0 = r_0 H \ \wedge\\\\\
C_1 = m G + r_1 \ek_1 \ \wedge\ D_1 = r_1 H
\end{array}
\right\\}
\end{align}

This is a _linear_ (homomorphic) relation over $\Gr$ in the scalar witnesses $(m, r_0, r_1) \in \F^3$, so it has a clean generalized-Schnorr / Chaum-Pedersen $\Sigma$-protocol.

### A $\Sigma$-protocol for $\Releq$

**Commit.** The prover samples masks $\alpha, \rho_0, \rho_1 \randget \F$ and sends:

* $A_0 \gets \alpha G + \rho_0 \ek_0$, &nbsp; $B_0 \gets \rho_0 H$
* $A_1 \gets \alpha G + \rho_1 \ek_1$, &nbsp; $B_1 \gets \rho_1 H$

**Challenge.** $c \randget \F$.

**Respond.** $z \gets \alpha + c \cdot m$, &nbsp; $s_0 \gets \rho_0 + c \cdot r_0$, &nbsp; $s_1 \gets \rho_1 + c \cdot r_1$ &nbsp;(all in $\F$).

**Verify.** Accept iff all four equations hold in $\Gr$:
\begin{align}
z G + s_0 \ek_0 &\overset{?}{=} A_0 + c \cdot C_0, & s_0 H &\overset{?}{=} B_0 + c \cdot D_0, \\\\\
z G + s_1 \ek_1 &\overset{?}{=} A_1 + c \cdot C_1, & s_1 H &\overset{?}{=} B_1 + c \cdot D_1.
\end{align}

This protocol is complete, special honest-verifier zero-knowledge, and 2-special-sound, hence a **proof of knowledge** of $(m, r_0, r_1)$.
The knowledge extractor is exactly what powers the simulation-soundness needed for CCA2.

### Fiat-Shamir $\Rightarrow$ NIZK

Bind the _entire_ statement into the challenge — this binding is what kills proof malleability:
\begin{align}
c \gets \Hash\big(\ek_0, \ek_1, C_0, D_0, C_1, D_1, A_0, B_0, A_1, B_1\big) \in \F
\end{align}

* $\nizk.\prove$ outputs $\pi \bydef (A_0, B_0, A_1, B_1, z, s_0, s_1)$.
* $\nizk.\verify$ recomputes $c$ from the statement and the $A_\bullet, B_\bullet$, then checks the four equations.

In the ROM, this is a one-time simulation-sound NIZK of knowledge, so the concrete scheme is **IND-CCA2**.

#### Why decryption can ignore $(C_1, D_1)$

A verified proof guarantees there exists $m$ with $C_0 - \dk_0 D_0 = m G = C_1 - \dk_1 D_1$.
So $\dk_0$ alone suffices for decryption, while the reduction's freedom to instead use $\dk_1$ — decrypting the _other_ ciphertext — is precisely what lets it simulate the decryption oracle without the challenge key.

## Remarks

* **Shorter proofs.** Subtracting the two ciphertexts eliminates $m$: $C_0 - C_1 = r_0 \ek_0 - r_1 \ek_1$. Proving the 2-generator relation in witnesses $(r_0, r_1)$ alone shrinks $\pi$ by one scalar.
* **Twisted variant.** If you prefer [Twisted ElGamal](/elgamal#twisted-elgamal) (so $C_0$ is a Pedersen commitment), swap the roles of $\ek$ and $H$ in the $D$-component; the $\Sigma$-protocol carries over unchanged.
